Poses

Forward bend Pose

In this pose, you bend forward from the hips, bringing your torso toward your legs. Benefits for the eyes include increased blood flow to the head, which can help reduce eye strain and promote relaxation.

Downward Facing Pose

This pose involves forming an inverted V-shape with your body, with your hands and feet on the ground and your hips lifted upward. Downward Dog can help relieve tension in the neck and shoulders, which can indirectly benefit eye health by reducing strain in surrounding muscles.

Thunderbolt Pose

This pose involves kneeling on the floor with your buttocks resting on your heels and your spine straight. This posture can help improve digestion and circulation, indirectly benefiting eye health by promoting overall well-being.

Extended Child's Pose

In this pose, you begin in Child's Pose (Balasana) and then extend your arms forward, stretching the spine and releasing tension in the back and neck. This relaxation can help alleviate eye strain caused by prolonged screen time or focusing on close objects.

Lotus Pose

This pose involves sitting cross-legged with each foot placed on the opposite thigh. It is a meditative posture that promotes calmness and focus. Regular practice of Lotus Pose can help reduce stress and improve mental clarity, which can indirectly benefit eye health by reducing tension and strain.
